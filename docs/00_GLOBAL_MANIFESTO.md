## The Context
The current "Cloud-First" healthcare model is brittle. It creates a dangerous dependency on third-party hyperscalers, subjects sensitive patient data to foreign jurisdiction, and collapses during infrastructure outages.

## The Four Tenets of Sovereignty

### 1. Clinical Autonomy (The Resilience Mandate)
A hospital that cannot function without an internet connection is not a sanctuary; it is a liability. Clinical logic must be resident in the ward. We prioritize **Offline-First** architecture to ensure that care never stops, even when the world goes dark.

### 2. Physical Privacy (The Residency Mandate)
Personal Health Information (PHI) is a sacred trust. Privacy is not an "option" to be toggled; it is a physical reality. In our OS, PII (Personally Identifiable Information) never leaves the physical building. We move the **Compute to the Data**, never the Data to the Cloud.
